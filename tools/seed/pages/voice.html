Section 04

Our copy moves. It writes from arrival. We're already doing the work, and the headline shows it: active, human, specific. Voice never changes. Tone flexes, warmer here, more measured there, while the point of view stays constant.

Verbs of Building

Lead with the verb of building.

Lead with

Build. Power. Advance. Accelerate. Shape. Design. Lead. Pursue.

Ease off

Educate, train, prove, demonstrate, validate — and hedges like "rising" or "becoming." They describe a school still trying to arrive. We've arrived.

Example headline

Power the return to the Moon.

Any university can have space programs. Only one was built for it. 29% of Kennedy Space Center runs on UCF degrees. Bold, specific, present — claim what's true.

  • Be specific. The exact fact beats the adjective every time.
  • Write from arrival. Lead with the verb of building. State what's already true.
  • Short & active. Short sentences. Active voice. Cut what isn't working.
  • Write for everyone. Accessible and translation-ready. Define any technical term in a few words.

Story Model

Not everything is a story — knowing which is which is the whole point. A story needs all four marks. Miss one and it's an update or an announcement, which can still matter, but it isn't a story.

  • Bold goal. A clear, ambitious aim that reaches past the ordinary.
  • Difficulty. Real obstacles. The easy path is never daring.
  • Transformation. Someone or something changes along the way.
  • Impact. A result that matters beyond the person at its center.

The test in one line

Doing is "I did something." Daring is "I did something that was hard and that mattered." If a piece can't say the second, it isn't a story.

Writer's block? Send your worst draft to a peer. Reacting to something bad is a hundred times easier than starting from nothing.

Lean In / Lean Away

Lean in

  • "29% of Kennedy Space Center's workforce holds a UCF degree."
  • "Build the next AI model in a lab that ships real research."

Lean away

  • "Innovative programs preparing you for the future."
  • "Learn AI fundamentals and gain valuable skills."

Need help with a headline? Stuck is normal. Trade drafts with another writer — a fresh read beats a blank page every time.
