Section 08

If it doesn't move a metric, it's decoration. We measure two things: whether the work performed, and whether the brand got stronger. Every page and every campaign gets one primary metric and a target, set at launch, not after.

What We Measure

Five tiers, from the fast signals to the slow, brand-deep ones. Pair a leading indicator with a lagging one so you're never fooled by a spike.

  • Tier 01 — Reach & attention. Impressions, reach, video views, sessions and new users, share of search. Did anyone see it?
  • Tier 02 — Engagement. Engagement rate, read/scroll depth, dwell time, CTR, saves and shares. Did it hold them? Weight shares over likes.
  • Tier 03 — Action & conversion. Applications, inquiries/RFIs, RSVPs, gifts, form completions — tied to each page's one job. Did it move them?
  • Tier 04 — Brand health. Aided & unaided awareness, favorability, sentiment, advocacy, and message association (do people tie UCF to innovation, space, scale?). Tracked by periodic survey.
  • Tier 05 — Search & discovery. Organic rankings, Search Console clicks & impressions, share of AI answer citations, Core Web Vitals. Are we findable?
  • Quality — the floor counts too. Accessibility conformance, page speed and error rates are metrics — a fast, accessible page is a performing page.

Standards

The basics.

  • One primary metric, set at launch. Every page and campaign names its single most important metric and a target before it ships. If everything matters, nothing does.
  • Instrument everything at launch. Analytics and Search Console connected on day one. Consistent UTM tags on every campaign link — one shared taxonomy, no freestyling.
  • Baseline before, measure after. No baseline, no claim. Capture the "before" so the "after" means something.
  • Report on a cadence. Dashboards monthly, a campaign wrap within two weeks of close, brand tracking at least annually. Trends over vanity absolutes.
  • Benchmark against yourself and peers. Compare to your own trend line first, then to peer institutions. Context turns a number into a finding.
  • Be honest about attribution. Correlation isn't cause. Name the confounders. Then feed what you learned back into the blocks.

Decide your one success metric before you build, and write it on the brief. It quietly settles half the design arguments before they start.

North Star

North star per output. Every format has one metric that tells the truth about it. Chase this one; treat the rest as diagnostics.
