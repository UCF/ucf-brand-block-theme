Section 03

The look, locked down. Our mark, our color and our type are the three things a person recognizes before they read a single word. They are not open to interpretation.

Logo

All of us are Knights. None of us are Pegasus. The Pegasus mark represents the university as an institution; the Knight represents our community. Use the institutional mark for official communications and never combine, recolor or reconstruct the two.

  • Maintain clear space equal to the height of the mark on all four sides.
  • Never stretch, rotate, outline or add effects to the mark.
  • Reproduce in Bold Gold on black, black on white, or white on a photograph with sufficient contrast.
  • Below 120px wide, switch to the stacked lockup.

Color

Bold Gold and UCF Black carry the brand. Horizon Blue is a digital-only accent for links, interface elements and annotations — it never appears in print. Everything else is a neutral in service of those three.

Contrast ratios below are measured against the stated background, not estimated.

Core palette

The one rule that breaks most often

Gold is a surface, not a text color

Bold Gold on white measures 1.83:1 — it fails every accessibility threshold there is. Use gold as a background carrying black text, as a graphic element on black, or as a rule and border. Never as body copy on a light background.

Typography

Three typefaces, three jobs. Display type is condensed and always uppercase. Body type carries every sentence a reader actually reads. Monospace is reserved for labels, eyebrows, meta and numbers — it never sets body copy.

Web alternates in use: Oswald stands in for URW DIN Condensed, Montserrat for Gotham. Print communications use the licensed originals.

Specimens

Type scale

Every size is fluid — it interpolates between a floor and a ceiling with the viewport, so there are no breakpoint jumps and no separate mobile scale to maintain.

Setting type

Do

  • Set headings in condensed display type, uppercase.
  • Keep measure at or under 68 characters.
  • Reserve monospace for eyebrows, labels and numbers.
  • Let the fluid scale handle small screens.

Don't

  • Set body copy in the display face.
  • Set paragraphs in all caps.
  • Introduce a fourth typeface for emphasis.
  • Hard-code pixel sizes outside the scale.

Graphic Elements

Square corners everywhere — no rounded rectangles. Gold reads as a rule, a left border or a top edge rather than a fill behind text. Hairline grids stand in for dividers, and halftone dot fields give dark sections texture without imagery.

  • Border radius is always zero.
  • Section tops may carry a 5px Bold Gold edge.
  • Active navigation is marked with a gold left border, not a background fill.
  • Focus rings are Horizon Blue on light, Bold Gold on dark — never removed.
