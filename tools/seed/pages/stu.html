Stu — brand review, score 82: mostly on-brand. 3 things to fix before this ships.

Ask a question

You: Can I put gold text on a white background?

Stu — short answer: no. Bold Gold on white lands around 1.8:1 contrast — below the WCAG 2.2 AA minimum, so it's hard to read and not compliant. Use gold as a background with black text, or on black. For text links on white, use the darkened Horizon Blue (5.9:1).

Checks

What Stu checks.

  • Logo & marks. Correct mark, clear space, approved color, no distortion.
  • Color & contrast. On-palette use, and every text pairing meets WCAG 2.2 AA.
  • Typography. Oswald / Montserrat (or approved alternates), sensible hierarchy.
  • Voice & tone. Verb-forward, specific, human. Flags lean-away and hedging.
  • Accessibility. Alt text, heading order, reading order, tap targets, link text.
  • Photography. Authentic, human, warm — and flagged for releases.
  • Claims & proof. Is the stat defensible, current and sourced? (E-E-A-T.)
  • Layout & grid. Alignment, spacing and the black-and-gold ratio.
  • A human still approves. Stu advises and speeds you up. Final sign-off stays with people.

How It Works

  • Add Stu where you work. Install the Stu app in Microsoft Teams, or call it from Copilot. No new tab, no new login.
  • Drop a file or ask a question. Attach a doc, slide, image or PDF — or just type your brand question in plain language.
  • Read the review. Get a scored, plain-English review: what passed, what to fix, and why — with the fastest fix first.
  • Apply the fixes — or ask Stu to. "Rewrite this headline." "Draft alt text." "Give me the compliant hexes." Stu drafts, you decide.
  • Escalate the edge cases. Unsure, or a new use the guide doesn't cover? Stu hands you off to Brand & Marketing with the context attached.

How Stu stays trustworthy

  • One guide. Stu answers only from this hub. No guessing, no outside opinions — if it's not in the guide, Stu says so.
  • Advises, doesn't approve. Stu flags and drafts. People make the call and own the sign-off.
  • Stays in our tenant. Files are reviewed inside UCF's Microsoft environment under existing data policies.

FAQ

Brand FAQs Stu can answer.

What's the primary logo now?

The stacked UCF is the primary mark; the horizontal wordmark supports it. Pegasus steps back. Use the stacked mark as given, from the official asset library — new work should use it, and you can retire old Pegasus-primary lockups as you refresh materials.

Can I use gold text on a white background?

No — Bold Gold on white fails WCAG 2.2 AA contrast. Use gold as a background with black text, or on black. For text links on white, use the darkened Horizon Blue.

Is my "ranked No. 1" claim okay to use?

If it's current and you can source it, yes — lead with it. UCF's voice is daring but defensible. Every claim needs to be provable; if you can't cite it, don't say it.

Which fonts should I use, and what are the web alternates?

Print uses Gotham and URW DIN Condensed. On the web, Oswald handles condensed display and Montserrat handles body/UI, with Arial as the everyday alternate. Use JetBrains Mono for technical labels.

Do I need the shared UCF header on my site?

Yes. Every official page loads the shared header from universityheader.ucf.edu — no exceptions, no custom rebuilds. It keeps identity and baseline navigation consistent everywhere.

Where do I get the stacked UCF files and templates?

From Resources — Adobe Express and PowerPoint templates, stationery, and MediaGraph for approved photography. For the new mark files or anything not covered, contact Brand & Marketing.
