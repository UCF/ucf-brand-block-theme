Section 05

Authentic, aspirational, human-centered. We photograph real UCF people doing real things, not stock-photo staging. Warm light, honest expressions, a sense of momentum. If it looks posed, it's not us.

Philosophy

Show people doing things. The work we make should look like the place it came from: students mid-work in a lab, candid and in natural light. Campus at golden hour, warm and aspirational. Motion with steady pacing and purposeful movement.

Shoot the moment right before the posed one. People laughing about getting set up almost always beats the picture they were setting up for.

Angel T. — University Photographer, Brand & Marketing

Standards

  • Subject — people, doing. Action over portrait. Show the work, the place, the result.
  • Light — warm & natural. Favor daylight and gold tones. Avoid flat, clinical lighting.
  • Composition — room to move. Give subjects space and direction. Let the frame breathe.
  • Post — true to life. Natural color, light contrast. No heavy filters or fake skies.

What to shoot

  • Real UCF people doing real things.
  • Warm light, honest expressions, a sense of momentum.
  • Candid action — the work, the place, the result.
  • Natural color and light contrast, true to life.

What not to shoot

  • Obvious stock setups and stiff, posed groups.
  • Empty, lifeless spaces with no human presence.
  • Over-filtered or heavily manipulated images.
  • Anything without proper model or location releases.

Resources

Approved photography and video live in MediaGraph, UCF's asset library. Request access through Brand & Marketing. Model and legal releases are required before any identifiable person is published. Drone and aerial capture follows campus airspace and safety guidance.
