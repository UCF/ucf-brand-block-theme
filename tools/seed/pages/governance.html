Section 09

Standards keep UCF's identity consistent and defensible. They're what lets thousands of people represent one university without it falling apart. Most of what follows is brand standards and best practices. Where the brand meets a binding university policy, we point you to the real thing.

  • Licensing & promo (standard). Merchandise, vendors and promotional items carrying UCF marks.
  • Brand governance (best practice). Who owns the brand, how exceptions are approved, how to request review.
  • AI guidelines (guideline). Using generative tools responsibly — with disclosure and human review.
  • Social media (standard). Standards for official accounts: voice, conduct, crisis response, approval.
  • Logo & mascot use (standard). Logo creation guidelines, plus how — and how not — to use the mascot.
  • Badges, rankings & accessibility (standard). Using rankings accurately and meeting accessibility obligations.

University Policies

The cards above are brand standards. Some sit on top of binding university policy — when something is a legal or institutional requirement, not just a brand preference, it lives at policies.ucf.edu. These are the ones most likely to touch your work:

  • 2-207 — Licensing and Use of University-owned Copyrighted Materials and Trademarks. Using and licensing UCF marks, names and logos.
  • 2-006 — Digital Accessibility. Accessibility requirements for digital content and websites.
  • 2-103 — Use of Copyrighted Material. Third-party photos, fonts, music and other media.
  • 6-002 — Public Information and Media Relations. Who speaks for UCF, and working with the media.
  • 3-133 — Unmanned Aircraft System (UAS/Drone). Drone and aerial capture for photo and video.
  • 3-104 — Display of U.S., State of Florida, and UCF Flags. Correct display of the UCF flag.
  • 4-002 — Use of Information Technologies and Resources. Acceptable use across UCF's digital platforms.

Policy numbers current as of this build — always confirm the latest version and full list at policies.ucf.edu. (Data handling for tools like Stu follows UCF's data policies, e.g. 4-008 Data Classification and Protection.)
